What Involved?

Overview

The Online Chinese Sentence Reading Study contains four experiments.

Experiment 1 and Experiment 2 are self-paced sentence reading tasks.

Experiment 3 and Experiment 4 include a self-paced sentence reading task plus a simple working memory capacity test. The memory test is the same in Experiments 3 and 4, so it only needs to be completed once if you do both.

A complete experiment includes
  1. a. Integrated Questionnaire: Chinese Learner Background and Two-Character Test (pre-screening, approx. 5 min; Experiment 1, 2, 3, 4)
  2. b. Online sentence reading task (main task, approx. 50 min for Experiment 1, 2; approx. 30 min for Experiment 3, 4)
  3. c. Working memory test (other task, approx. 20 min; only for Experiment 3, 4)

Completion time may vary slightly based on individual reading speed and breaks.

Brief Outline of Tasks

Pre-screening (a)

In the pre-screening stage, you will complete a questionnaire designed to assess your Chinese learning background and vocabulary size. (Experiment 1, 2, 3, 4)

Main online session (b)

In the main online session, you will take part in a self-paced sentence reading task. You will read a series of sentences and answer simple Yes/No comprehension questions.

The task is conducted entirely online and requires no software installation, allowing you to complete it flexibly in a quiet environment. (Experiment 1, 2, 3, 4)

Working memory (c)

In the final stage, you will complete an Automated Operation Span Task to assess your working memory capacity. This is a dual-task paradigm that involves remembering a sequence of letters while simultaneously verifying simple math equations. (only for Experiment 3, 4)

Privacy & Confidentiality

Your privacy is our utmost priority. All data collected will be strictly confidential, fully anonymised, and used solely for academic research purposes.

To protect your identity, any information that could directly identify you (such as your name) will be removed and replaced with an anonymous code. The key linking this code to your personal details will be stored securely and separately from the research data. All personal information will be destroyed five years after the project's completion.

Anonymised data may be made openly available online via a trusted repository (e.g., the Open Science Framework, https://osf.io/) to support future research. This study has been reviewed by the University Research Ethics Committee and has been given a favourable ethical opinion for conduct. Participation in this study is entirely voluntary, and you may withdraw at any time without providing a reason.
