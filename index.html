About the Study

Welcome to our online study. We are an academic research team from School of English Studies, Beijing International Studies University. This research investigates how native English speakers comprehend and process Chinese sentences. Your participation will support high-impact publications and advance linguistic research and international Chinese education.

Funding: National Social Science Fund of China

Investigators: Prof. Changyin Zhou & Dr. Huan Liu

Contact for Queries: liuhuanpsylanguage@foxmail.com

What Involved?

  • a. Integrated Questionnaire: Chinese Learner Background and Two-Character Test (pre-screening, approx. 5 min)
  • b. Online sentence reading task (main task, approx. 30 min)
  • c. Working memory test (other task, approx. 20 min)

Completion time may vary slightly based on individual reading speed and breaks.

Brief Outline of Tasks

All tasks are conducted online, without requiring any software installation, and can be completed in a quiet environment.

a. In the pre-screening stage, you will complete a questionnaire designed to assess your Chinese learning background and vocabulary size.

b. In the main online session, you will take part in a self-paced sentence reading task. You will read a series of sentences and answer simple Yes/No comprehension questions.

c. In the final stage, you will complete an Automated Operation Span Task to assess your working memory capacity. This is a dual-task paradigm that involves remembering a sequence of letters while simultaneously verifying simple math equations.

Privacy & Confidentiality

Your privacy is our utmost priority. All data collected will be strictly confidential, fully anonymised, and used solely for academic research purposes. To protect your identity, any information that could directly identify you (such as your name) will be removed and replaced with an anonymous code. The key linking this code to your personal details will be stored securely and separately from the research data. All personal information will be destroyed five years after the project's completion. Anonymised data may be made openly available online via a trusted repository (e.g., the Open Science Framework, https://osf.io/) to support future research. This study has been reviewed by the University Research Ethics Committee and has been given a favourable ethical opinion for conduct. Participation in this study is entirely voluntary, and you may withdraw at any time without providing a reason.
